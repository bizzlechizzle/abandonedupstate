### Factory Background

The Fair Play Factory in West Endicott, New York, was established by the Endicott-Johnson Corporation, the nation’s leading shoe manufacturer and a visible champion of welfare capitalism. The sprawling complex served as one of the company’s primary Fair Play facilities, reinforcing Endicott-Johnson’s reputation for both production scale and employee-focused amenities.

### Opening Day Momentum

Although construction was still wrapping up, the factory officially opened on Monday, June 4, 1923. A News Dispatch article published that April noted workers were already building shoes inside the unfinished plant. At full speed the Fair Play Factory aimed to staff more than 1,000 employees and turn out 7,000 pairs of shoes every day.

### Vacancy and Demolition

Production slowed in the ensuing decades and the facility eventually sat idle by the 1970s. After decades of disuse, the Fair Play Factory site was cleared in October 2018 to make room for National Pipe & Plastics’ new headquarters.

### Key Dates

- **April 1923** – Local press reports production underway prior to completion.
- **June 4, 1923** – Formal opening with capacity for 1,000 workers and 7,000 shoes daily.
- **1970s** – Complex falls vacant as Endicott-Johnson’s regional footprint contracts.
- **October 2018** – Buildings demolished for National Pipe & Plastics redevelopment.
